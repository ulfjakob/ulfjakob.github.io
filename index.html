Ulf Jakob Flø Aarsnes, Ph.D.

Modeling, estimation, and control for drilling and energy systems

I develop model-based methods for nonlinear and distributed-parameter systems, with emphasis on deployment-oriented control and estimation. My recent work combines drilling automation with energy-management problems for low-emission systems.

ulfjakob@gmail.com · LinkedIn · Google Scholar

Current Research Focus

Drilling dynamics and automation

  • Axial and torsional vibration mechanisms in long horizontal wells.
  • Model-based mitigation, friction estimation, and startup trajectory design.
  • Flow and pressure control using reduced two-phase models.

Energy systems and control

  • Online energy management for fuel-cell and battery hybrid systems.
  • Optimization and supervisory control for efficient, robust operation.
  • Transfer of control-theoretic tools from drilling to low-emission systems.

Selected Work Streams

Drill-string vibrations

Analysis and control-oriented models for stick-slip and coupled axial-torsional effects, including field validation and industrially relevant mitigation settings.

Real-time optimization while drilling

Extremum-seeking and transient-aware optimization of weight on bit and drilling efficiency, supported by both theory and laboratory validation.

Flow, pressure, and state estimation

Simplified multiphase models and estimation frameworks designed for practical controller synthesis and real-time operational use.
